Cortina is a ski town, not a culinary destination. But there's good food here if you know where to look. The mountain huts (rifugi) often serve better, more honest food than the fancy hotel restaurants. Traditional Ampezzo cuisine leans Tyrolean: dumplings, game, polenta, hearty alpine fare perfect after a day on the slopes.

The Best of Cortina

Tivoli

Michelin Star Reserve Wine

Chef Graziano Prest's refined take on Dolomite cuisine. The only Michelin star in Cortina, but not pretentious - warm alpine atmosphere with views. Menu showcases local ingredients with sophisticated technique. Worth the splurge for a special dinner.

Località Lacedel, 34 | €100+/person | +39 0436 866400 | Opens 7pm

Al Camin

Local Favorite Reserve Wine

Chef Fabio Pompanin's cozy restaurant in Alverà. Consistently rated the best "everyday" fine dining in Cortina by locals. Traditional dishes executed perfectly - casunziei, game, dumplings. Wooden interiors, fireplace, exactly what you want on a cold mountain evening.

Località Alverà, 99 | €50-70/person | +39 0436 862010 | Tue-Sun 12pm & 7pm

Mountain Huts (Rifugi)

The soul of Dolomite dining. Reach these by ski lift or snowshoe, eat hearty alpine food with stunning views.

Rifugio Scoiattoli

Cinque Torri Views Walk-in Wine

At the base of the iconic Cinque Torri rock towers. Cozy rooms with ceramic stoves, large sun terrace for lunch. High-quality food and wines, surprisingly refined for a mountain hut. Perfect for a ski lunch with one of the best views in the Dolomites.

Cinque Torri, 2137m | €25-40/person | Lift access from Bai de Dones

Rifugio Averau

Panoramic Family-Run

Traditional recipes passed down through generations - try the "Cappelli d'Alpino" pasta or the pheasant timbale. Historic rifugio with incredible 360° Dolomite views. More rustic than Scoiattoli, equally memorable.

Averau, 2413m | €20-35/person | rifugioaverau.it

In Town - Casual

Il Vizietto di Cortina

Walk-in OK Local Crowd

Roman pastas in the Dolomites. When you want a break from dumplings and game, this is your spot for proper carbonara, amatriciana, cacio e pepe. Central location on Corso Italia, always buzzing. Over 3,000 Google reviews and still maintaining quality.

Corso Italia, 53 | €20-40/person | +39 0436 867369 | Opens 12pm

Restaurant Ra Stua

Reserve Traditional

Classic Cortina institution. Known for exceptional beef and traditional Ampezzo dishes. Old-school service, wood-paneled rooms, the kind of place with regulars who've been coming for decades. Family warmth mentioned in every review.

Via Grohmann, 2 | €40-60/person | +39 0436 868341 | Tue-Sun 12pm & 7pm

Pontejel Bierstube Forst

Walk-in

Don't let the "bierstube" name fool you - this isn't a loud beer hall. Quiet restaurant serving excellent Tyrolean and Veneto cuisine with great Forst beer. Central location, reasonable prices, locals' go-to for a simple good meal.

Centro | €25-40/person | Tue-Sun

Notes for Alejandra

All selections are pregnancy-safe. The mountain cuisine here is naturally pregnancy-friendly - cooked meats, dumplings, polenta, well-done game. Avoid: any tartare if offered, raw-milk cheeses unless you're comfortable, Spritz (obviously). The rifugi tend to be well-heated but bring layers.
